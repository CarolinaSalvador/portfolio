UX/UI Design

Whole recipes

CASE STUDY

Category

UX/UI Design

Whole recipes is a mobile aplication concept that combines dietary needs with day-to-day requirements to offer a selection of recipes completely adapted to any moment. Users can look for a recipe according to their preferences, and follow a step-by-step guide supported with a short video to prepare the meal.

WHAT WE ALREADY KNOW

Here we have some important facts regarding how dietary habits are changing in Spain.

  • Plant-based diets are growing across the entire world, and indeed in Spain.

  • Around an 8% of the spanish population over the age of 18 years consider themselves as flexitarians.

  • The number of vegan people has exponentially increased over the past years.

  • The number of celiacs diagnosed in Spain increases a 15% per year.

  • Feeding intolerance has become a public health problem that affects more than two million people in Spain.

RESEARCH

The research was based on surveys, interviews and investigation about dietary problems, behaviors and users needs. Before conducting the interviews, I listed out potential questions that may be asked during the session. These questions were centered on the following topics.

Over 65 people participated and their answers stablished a key pattern to bring the research to the next stage. More than 25% of the people had a disease or a special dietary requirement, the majority were concerned about food and already used tools to help themselves improve their habits.

Analyzing the research results some patterns and pain points came to light:

  1. Users feel frustated because dietary needs limit the access to all kind of recipes.

  2. They want to eat well and have good habits but the lack of time and habits already acquired make them fall into routine.

  3. users are interested in searching for recipes but sometimes they find difficult to put it into practice because they don't understand the cooking process and these recipes have a lot of ingredients hard to find in the closest supermarket.

  4. The lack of ideas is a big matter, they need a clear guidance where to start. Simplicity wins.

USER PERSONA

Based on the the previous research, we can introduce Lucía. She is 32, lives in Madrid and works full time in an advertising agency as an Email Marketing Manager. She is a woman of firm beliefs who cares for her feed and has been following a veggie diet for ten years. Lucía usually cooks at home and brings her meal to work, this way ensures eating well and saves money. But it's not always like that, sometimes she finds difficult to eat healthier because overwork and the lack of time and ideas makes her have a poorly balanced diet and fall into a feed routine.

DEFINING THE PROBLEM: problem statement

At this point, the problem has already arisen. Lucía, that follows a specific diet and usually cooks at home, needs new and fresh recipe ideas, not very complex and with easy to find ingredients, so eating a healthful diet according to her preferences becomes easier.

SOLVING THE PROBLEM: solving statement

The idea is to provide recipes completely adapted to the user on a mobile application because it's easier to use in the kitchen. The user needs to be at the beginning of the research process, taking decisions to avoid future frustations. These decisions will include selecting restricted ingredients and choosing dietary needs when signing up. Also, when preparing the meal, users can filter by level of difficulty of the recipe, time available for cooking and ingredients on hand, so the app can display an accurate research according to their current needs.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

In order to understand how a user interacts with the app to perform tasks, let's illustrate the sitemap. It was esential to keep it simple, as if the user had used it before. To achieve this goal a deep navigation was avoided, and the focus was placed on the main feature: look for recipes that match the dietary needs and preferences of the user.

WIREFRAMES

The next step was to create low-fidelity wireframes of the app in order to be able to test with users the sooner the better, and have feedback of the whole process done so far. The fedback received in the session was very useful to simplify the flow and to avoid distractinfg elements that weren't essential for the MVP that was being created. This process allowed me to move formard to the next step, to develop a high-fidelity prototye.

HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

This high-fidelity prototype based on the feedback received helped me and the users to have a better idea of the actual imagery. At this point, a new test was conducted and this time, I gathered more positive feedback. It's always good to test changes and have new feedback that will make the project go in the right direction.

SIGN UP WORKFLOW

The user can choose either to sign up in the app or not. If decides to do it, the app will ask which type of diet is following to avoid those ingredients in the suggested recipes. The app will also ask if there's any extra ingredient that the user wants to avoid. these preferences will automatically be saved and could be changed at any time from the profile area.

SEARCH TOOL & FAVOURITES WORKFLOW

The home screen provides a clear direction where to start. And the search tool is located here. All kind of suggestion are displayed according to the preferences that the user set up when siging up. If the user didn't do the enrollment the home screen will show up recipes based on most popular categories, best rated or random occasions.

The user can search for recipes quickly and easy and get relevant results using auto complete but even more, he can refine the search using the filters. The level of the recipe can be set up here, the time available for cooking and the ingredients that the recipe should include because the user has them on hand.

PROFILE WORKFLOW

The user has access to this area from the navbar manu. It is easy to change the initial preferences set up when signing up from here.
